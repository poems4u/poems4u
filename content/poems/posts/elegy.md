title: "Elegy For Coach Davis"
date: 2026-03-02T00:00:00
---

Like a racehorse, they took my hero out back: <br>  
Undignified and thankless <br>  
And left the train a-steaming <br>  
And all fell off the tracks <br>  

Like a wolf, his house destroyed and paved: <br>  
Centuries-old love <br>  
Was uprooted for a future <br>  
Where no sun lit the way <br>  

Like Lazarus, rolled away to rest: <br>  
Gaping, gorged, the throng, <br>  
Who couldn’t parse <br>  
What the weeping missed <br>  

What if it was the case <br>  
That beloved John had lied? <br>  
What if old Lazarus really <br>  
Stayed dead when he had died? <br>  

What if our dear Lord <br>  
Never really came? <br>  
What if Martha stayed there crying <br>  
While the Jews cursed on his name? <br>  

And what if He was there and did call <br>  
“Lazarus, come out!” <br>  
And nothing shifted, nothing stirred, <br>  
The dead man stayed his route <br>  

If, only after stillness <br>  
Was when Jesus really wept—— <br>  
And the others blinked their eyes <br>  
While the peaceful departed slept <br>  

If, at that moment, the stench poured out <br>  
And laid waste upon the air, <br>  
Mouths covered, feet scattered, <br>  
While Mary rent her hair <br>  

If loyalty and family, honor and love <br>  
Were sold off for a purse, <br>  
And hopes and dreams, sighs and songs <br>  
Drove off in a hearse?
