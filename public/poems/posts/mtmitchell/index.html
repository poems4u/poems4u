Assault on Mt. Mitchell

I’m more than capable of navigation,
So I was there at the base——
Regarding a conifer carpet of black.

I pushed down the sign
And ravaged the accommodations.

My hands were plunged into the deep earth;
I crawled, like a wild animal,
Ascending and casting down
What debris,
Panting, vomiting: voracious.

I fell prostrate at the summit
And felt the wind pass above me.

The firs craned to see the achievement
Of a staggered stance.

I stepped over Elisha;
I ordered the flies to disperse,

And once I got to where I could
See you all, attending to
Your little dreams,

It was to no one in particular,
(Addressé au monde)
and with no locomotive consideration,
I hope you realize:

I’ll fuck you till you love me.
