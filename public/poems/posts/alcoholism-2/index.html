Alcoholism 2 (Le Grand Verre)

When faced with loss that’s one’s own fault
It’s oft facile to plead
For those projections quickly sought
And then considered needs.

Alas,,,

And
With apologies to Conner——
Soon will carry disease,
So many parts——why bother
With
“How many?"——“at least three!”

One handed beer, cig in the other,
My heart on two-way streets
Just one-sided traffic covers——
Simply salty or sweet.

But let’s get real…

Beauty in men results from the interplay between soft angles and curves.

Life is so frustrating because all beings are at once extractors and ever-divisible zones of extraction

To be cricked and cracked and crooked——

To be is to No
and to Know is to kick and scream——

To throw away that necessary etheric force
In water and in heat.

And when I drink vodka my head sounds like this:
